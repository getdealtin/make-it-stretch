And Why We Built It

Every number on this site comes from somewhere real. Every design decision was made with someone in mind. Here's how it all works — no fine print, no spin.

Data Sources

📊 Here's how food prices work in this tool

Every price you see comes from the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS) Average Price Data — the same source the federal government uses to measure what Americans pay for groceries. The BLS sends data collectors into stores across the country every month to record actual shelf prices. Those prices are averaged by region and published publicly.

When you enter your zip code, we map it to one of four BLS geographic regions — Northeast, Midwest, South, or West — and pull the prices for that region. This gets you closer to your local reality than a national average would.

📍 BLS Average Price Data 🔄 Updated monthly 🆓 Free public data
The honest tradeoff: BLS prices are regional averages — not your specific store. Your store brand may cost less. A sale could stretch this further. We chose BLS because it's free, verified, and never disappears behind a paywall. Major grocery chains don't share live pricing through open public data.

🥦 Here's how we calculate calories per dollar

For each staple food, we calculate calories per dollar — how many calories you get for every dollar spent. This ranks your shopping list by the most food for your money first, not by what's trendy or what a nutritionist would ideally recommend.

Calorie data comes from the USDA FoodData Central database — the government's official nutritional reference. We use standard serving sizes and package weights consistent with what you'd actually buy at a store.

📍 USDA FoodData Central 🆓 Free public data
Why calories? When a budget is very tight, calories are survival math. We also show estimated calories per person per day so you can see whether your plan meets basic nutritional needs — and where to seek additional resources if it doesn't.

🏛 Here's how we find your federal representatives

Your zip code maps to a US state. We use that state to look up your two US Senators from a curated dataset sourced from Congress.gov — the official database of the US Legislature, maintained by the Library of Congress.

For US House Representatives, congressional districts are drawn at a level more granular than zip codes allow us to resolve accurately. We link directly to the official House.gov lookup tool so you can find your exact representative in one click.

📍 Congress.gov Members 📍 House.gov Lookup
On voting records: Federal vote records shown are roll-call votes from the 119th Congress (2025), specifically bills with direct impact on SNAP, food assistance, and the Farm Bill. Roll-call votes are the only votes tracked in official congressional data — voice votes and procedural votes are not recorded by member.

🏟 Here's how we find your state representatives

State legislative data comes from OpenStates (Plural Policy) — an open civic data project that aggregates legislative information from all 50 states, Washington D.C., and Puerto Rico. It standardizes state legislature data that would otherwise require visiting 50 different state websites.

When you enter your zip code, we query OpenStates for your state legislators and search for recent bills related to food costs, grocery taxes, SNAP state funding, and nutrition assistance. Results show how your state legislators voted on those bills.

📍 OpenStates / Plural Policy 🔄 Updated continuously 🆓 Free public API
Coverage note: OpenStates coverage varies by state. Some states provide more complete voting records than others. Where data is incomplete, we link directly to your state legislature's official website.

Honest Tradeoffs

⚠ Why we don't show live store prices

Major grocery chains — Walmart, Kroger, Aldi, Target — don't share live pricing through open public APIs. Their pricing data is commercially sensitive. Third-party scrapers exist but operate in legal gray areas and can break without warning. We chose BLS government data because it's stable, verified, and free — even if it means regional averages instead of aisle-level prices.

⚠ Why voting records show party patterns, not always individual votes

Some votes — especially state-level — don't have complete individual member records in public databases. Where individual records aren't available, we show the majority position of each party on that vote and link to the official record. We never invent or assume a vote. If we don't know, we say so and link out.

⚠ Why we can't identify your exact House district from a zip code

Congressional districts don't align neatly with zip codes. A single zip code can span multiple districts, and a single district can contain parts of many zip codes. Accurate district lookup requires geocoding (converting your address to coordinates) which requires either a paid API or your precise address — more than we ask for. We link to the official House.gov lookup rather than guess.

Your Privacy

🔒 Here's exactly what happens with your information

  • Nothing is stored by default. Everything you enter runs in your browser. When you close the page, it's gone.
  • No account required. You get your full results without creating anything or logging in.
  • No ads. We don't run ads. We don't let advertisers target you based on what you search here.
  • No data selling. We will never sell your information to anyone. Ever.
  • If you choose to save your data — using Google or Apple sign-in — we keep only what's listed below. You can delete it anytime, instantly.

If you choose to save (opt-in only):
✓ Your zip code · ✓ Household headcount · ✓ Budget bracket · ✓ Tool usage frequency

What we never keep, no matter what:
✕ Your name · ✕ Your exact dollar amount · ✕ Specific meal configuration history · ✕ Personally identifiable footprints

What saved data is used for: Anonymized and aggregated only. We combine data from all users to show patterns — like "In zip codes 38103–38115, the average household budget for two weeks of groceries is $31." That aggregate data is shared with policymakers and researchers to show what the affordability crisis actually looks like for real people. Your data is never linked back to you individually.
