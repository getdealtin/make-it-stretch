📊 Where this data comes from — and what it can't do

Prices come from the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics — the same source the federal government uses to track what Americans pay for food. Nutritional data from USDA FoodData Central. Updated regularly.

The honest tradeoff: Major grocery chains don't share live pricing through open public data. BLS regional averages are close — but your store brand may cost less and a sale could stretch this further. Use this as a strong starting point, then adjust in the aisle.
