📊 Where this data comes from — and what it can't do

Prices come from the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS) Average Price Data — the same source the federal government uses to track what Americans pay for food. Updated monthly. Your zip code maps to a BLS region for the closest available estimate.

The honest tradeoff: Major grocery chains like Walmart and Kroger don't share live pricing through open public data. BLS regional averages are close — but your store brand may cost less, and a sale could stretch this further. Use this as a strong starting point, then adjust in the aisle.

Why BLS and not store prices? Free. Transparent. Government-verified. Never paywalled. We'd rather give you trustworthy approximate prices than exact prices we can't guarantee.
